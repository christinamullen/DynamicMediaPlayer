ITALIAN STUFFED PEPPERS

My Grandmother was the best cook, but she never measured or used a cookbook. This was sometimes hard to follow and produced inconsistent results. Here I provide a traditional (yet precise) recipe for Stuffed Peppers.

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Spray a roasting pan (large enough to accommodate 8 pepper halves in a single layer) with cooking spray.
  2. Combine the ground sirloin, Italian sausage, brown rice, Italian seasonings, salt, pepper, garlic, Parmesan cheese, egg, and 1/2 cup garlic and basil tomato sauce in a large bowl. Mix until combined.
  3. Divide meat mixture evenly among the green pepper halves; press to compress slightly. Arrange halves in prepared pan. Spoon the remaining garlic and basil tomato sauce onto the tops of the meat and peppers. Cover with lid.
  4. Bake until the peppers are soft and internal temperature of the meat reaches 165 degrees F, about 1 1/2 hours.
  5. Remove from oven. Remove the lid and top each pepper with shredded cheese.
  6. Turn oven to broil. Place peppers, uncovered under the broiler. Broil until the cheese is melted and bubbly, about 5 minutes. Sprinkle with parsley.
