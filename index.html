ITALIAN STUFFED PEPPERS

My Grandmother was the best cook, but she never measured or used a cookbook. This was sometimes hard to follow and produced inconsistent results. Here I provide a traditional (yet precise) recipe for Stuffed Peppers.

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Start by preparing the bell peppers, boil them for two minutes to cut down on cooking time.
  3. Boil the peppers for two minutes to cut down the baking time in the oven
  4. Slice off the tops of the peppers, remove the seeds, then replace the "lids" of the peppers
  5. In a frying pan, pour a drizzle of olive oil and add the onion and garlic to fry for about two minutes or until golden.
  6. For the stuffing: In a large bowl, mix the pork, fried onion and garlic, cheese, marjoram, parsley, salt and pepper,
  7. Then add about 3/4 of the rice and 1 cup of the tomato sauce and mix together.
  8. In a baking dish, pour the rest of the tomato sauce into the dish and make sure to move the dish around so that the sauce covers the bottom evenly.
  9. Scoop a handful of the stuffing into each pepper, filling them to the top and then place onto the baking dish.
  10. When done filling, place the pepper tops back on top of each pepper. This helps to cook the stuffing evenly.
  11. Place the pan in the oven and bake the stuffed peppers for about one hour.
  12. Once they are done, remove from the oven and serve with some of the sauce.
